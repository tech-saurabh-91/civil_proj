## 1. How This App Works — The Big Picture

### What is BuildSurvey Pro?

This app manages the **entire life of a construction project** — from the moment someone shows interest (Lead) → to signing them as a client → to surveying their site → to giving them a price quote → to managing the project until completion.

### The Business Flow (Memorize This)

```
LEAD → CLIENT → PROJECT → SURVEY → BOQ → QUOTATION → WORK → REPORT → SIGN-OFF
```

**In simple words:**

1. **Someone calls you** saying they want construction work done → That's a **Lead**
2. **You sign them up** as a customer → That becomes a **Client**
3. **You create a Project** for their work → That's a **Project**
4. **Your engineer visits the site** and inspects everything → That's a **Survey**
5. **You calculate costs** item by item → That's a **BOQ**
6. **You send them a price** → That's a **Quotation**
7. **Work happens** → You track risks, materials, documents
8. **You generate reports** and get signatures → Project complete

### Who Uses What?

| Person | Primary Modules |
|--------|----------------|
| **Sales/BD Team** | Leads, Clients, Quotations |
| **Project Manager** | Projects, Surveys, Workflows, Reports |
| **Site Engineer** | Surveys (field), Measurements, Photos, GPS |
| **Surveyor** | Survey execution, Checklists, Media capture |
| **Accountant** | BOQ, Quotations, Cost Estimation |
| **Admin** | Users, Roles, Settings, Masters |

## Step 1: Get a Lead

### What is a Lead?

A **Lead** is any person or company that has shown interest in hiring you for construction work. They found your website, someone referred them, you met them at a trade show, or they called your office.

> **Real Example:** A builder named "Rajesh Mehta" from "Sunrise Builders" calls your office saying: "We are starting a new residential project in Powai, Mumbai. We need a site survey and structural assessment. Can you send us a quote?"

That phone call = **ONE LEAD** in your system.

### When to Create a Lead?

- Someone calls or emails asking about your services
- You get a business card at an event
- Someone fills the "Contact Us" form on your website
- A existing client refers someone to you
- You find a potential customer on LinkedIn

### How to Create a Lead

Go to **CRM → Leads → + New Lead**

#### Step 1: Contact Information

| Field | What to Enter | Example | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **Full Name** | Contact person's name | Rajesh Mehta | This is who you will talk to |
| **Email** | Their email address | rajesh@sunrisebuilders.com | For sending quotations and follow-ups |
| **Phone** | Mobile or office number (min 10 digits) | 9876543210 | For calls and WhatsApp communication |
| **Company** | Their company name | Sunrise Builders Pvt. Ltd. | Identifies which organization |
| **Website** | Company website (if any) | https://sunrisebuilders.com | Helps you research the company |

> **Tip:** Always get the phone number — you will use WhatsApp and calls to communicate throughout the project.

#### Step 2: Lead Details

| Field | What to Enter | Options | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **Lead Source** | How did they find you? | Website, Referral, LinkedIn, Cold Call, Exhibition/Trade Show, Partner, Social Media, Other | Tells you which marketing channel works |
| **Status** | Current stage of the deal | NEW → CONTACTED → QUALIFIED → PROPOSAL_SENT → NEGOTIATION → WON | Tracks your sales pipeline |

## Step 4: Do a Site Survey

### What is a Survey?

A **Survey** is a detailed on-site inspection where your engineer visits the construction site, checks everything (structure, electrical, plumbing, safety), takes photos, records GPS location, measures dimensions, and creates a comprehensive report.

> **This is the CORE of your business.** This is what clients pay you for.

### When to Do a Survey?

- **Before construction starts** — Initial survey to understand site conditions
- **During construction** — Periodic inspection to check quality
- **Before handover** — Final survey to verify everything is done right
- **When problems arise** — Follow-up survey to investigate issues

### How to Create a Survey

Go to **Surveys → + New Survey**

The survey has **7 steps**. Fill each step carefully.

---

#### Step 1: Project Selection

| Field | What to Enter | Example | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **Project** | Which project is this survey for? | Sunrise Enclave - Structural Survey | Links survey to project |

| **Survey Title** | Descriptive title | Foundation Inspection - Phase 1 | Identifies this specific survey |
| **Description** | What will you check? | "Inspect foundation depth, soil condition, and rebar placement" | Clarity for the engineer |

**Survey Types — When to Use Each:**

| Type | When | What You Do |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Initial Survey** | First visit to a new site | Baseline data: GPS, photos, measurements, site conditions. Everything is new. |
| **Detailed Survey** | Deep inspection of specific area | Focus on one thing: structural integrity, electrical systems, etc. |
| **Follow-up Survey** | Revisiting to check progress | Compare with previous survey. Did the issues get fixed? |
| **Final Survey** | Before handing over to client | Comprehensive check of everything. Final report. |
| **As-Built Survey** | After construction is complete | Document what was actually built vs what was planned. |

## Step 5: Create BOQ

### What is BOQ?

**BOQ = Bill of Quantities**. It is a detailed list of ALL construction items needed for the project, with quantities and rates. This is how you calculate the total project cost.

> **Think of it like a grocery bill** — but for construction. Instead of "rice, dal, oil", you have "concrete, bricks, steel, labor".

### When to Create BOQ?

- After the initial survey is complete (you need measurements)
- Before sending a quotation to the client
- When project scope changes

### How to Create

Go to **BOQ → + Add Item**

#### BOQ Item Fields

| Field | What to Enter | Example | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **Description** | What is this item? | Earthwork excavation in foundation trenches | What you are charging for |
| **Category** | Type of work | Earthwork | Grouping and subtotals |
| **Unit** | Measurement unit | Cum (cubic meters) | Standard unit of measurement |
| **Quantity** | How much? | 450 | Comes from survey measurements |
| **Rate (INR)** | Price per unit in ₹ | 350 | Your rate for this work |
| **Amount** | Auto-calculated | ₹1,57,500 | Quantity × Rate |

**Categories:**

| Category | Items That Go Here |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Earthwork** | Excavation, backfilling, soil disposal |
| **Concrete** | PCC, RCC, ready-mix concrete |
| **Masonry** | Brickwork, blockwork, stone masonry |
| **Finishing** | Plastering, painting, tiling |
| **Plumbing** | Pipes, fittings, fixtures |
| **Electrical** | Wiring, switches, panels |
| **HVAC** | Air conditioning, ventilation |

**Units Explained:**

| Unit | Full Form | Use For | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Cum** | Cubic Meter | Volume (concrete, earthwork) | 450 Cum of excavation |
| **Sqm** | Square Meter | Area (plastering, painting) | 2000 Sqm of plastering |
| **Rmt** | Running Meter | Length (pipes, cables) | 500 Rmt of piping |
| **Nos** | Numbers | Countable items | 100 Nos of doors |
| **Set** | Set | Complete assemblies | 1 Set of elevator |
| **Mtr** | Meter | Length | 100 Mtr of fencing |
| **Bags** | Bags | Cement | 500 Bags of cement |
| **Tonnes** | Tonnes | Heavy materials | 50 Tonnes of steel |

> **The BOQ groups items by category** and shows subtotals for each group, plus a grand total with 18% GST.

## Step 6: Send Quotation to Client

### What is a Quotation?

A **Quotation** (also called "quote" or "estimate") is a formal document you send to the client showing:
- What work you will do
- How much it will cost
- Terms and conditions
- How long the price is valid

> **This is what the client sees.** Make it professional.

### When to Send?

- After BOQ is ready
- After initial survey is complete
- When client asks "how much will it cost?"

### How to Create

Go to **Quotations → + New Quotation**

#### Step 1: Project & Details

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Select Project** | Which project? | Sunrise Enclave - Structural Survey |
| **Quotation Title** | Descriptive title | Site Survey & Structural Assessment Quotation |
| **Description** | What work is included | "Comprehensive structural survey including foundation, column, beam, slab inspection with GPS mapping and detailed report" |

#### Step 2: Line Items

Each line item is one piece of work you are charging for.

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Description** | What is this item? | Initial Site Survey with GPS Mapping |
| **Unit** | Measurement unit | Set |
| **Quantity** | How many? | 1 |
| **Rate (INR)** | Price per unit | 75000 |
| **Amount** | Auto-calc | ₹75,000 |

> **Tip:** Add multiple line items. Example:
> 1. Initial Site Survey - Set × 1 × ₹75,000 = ₹75,000
> 2. Soil Testing - Nos × 5 × ₹8,000 = ₹40,000
> 3. Structural Inspection Report - Set × 1 × ₹25,000 = ₹25,000
> **Subtotal = ₹1,40,000**

#### Step 3: Tax & Terms

| Field | What to Enter | Example | Why |
|-------|---------------|---------|-----|
| **GST %** | Which GST rate? | 18% | Government tax |
| **Discount %** | Any discount? | 5% | Client relationship |
| **Terms & Conditions** | Legal terms | Pre-filled with 8 standard terms | Protection for both parties |

> **GST on construction services is typically 18%.** The system auto-calculates:
> - Subtotal: ₹1,40,000
> - Discount (5%): -₹7,000
> - After Discount: ₹1,33,000
> - GST (18%): ₹23,940
> - **Grand Total: ₹1,56,940**

## Step 9: Workflow & Approvals

### What is a Workflow?

A **Workflow** is an approval chain. When a survey or report needs approval, it goes through a workflow:

```
Engineer submits → Manager reviews → Client approves → Done
```

### Workflow Step Types

| Type | What It Does | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Approval** | Someone must approve to proceed | Survey approval, BOQ approval |
| **Review** | Someone examines and gives feedback | Technical review, quality check |
| **Notification** | Sends alert to stakeholders | "Survey submitted, please review" |
| **Condition** | Different path based on criteria | If budget > ₹10L → go to senior manager |
| **Parallel** | Multiple people review at same time | Manager + Client review simultaneously |

### Digital Signatures

**What:** Electronic signature for official approvals.

**How to Sign:**
1. Click the signature area
2. **Draw** with mouse or finger (touch screen)
3. **Type** your name in signature font
4. **Upload** an image of your handwritten signature
